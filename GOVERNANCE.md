# Governance

Hardproof is maintainer-led through v1.0.0. Maintainers set release scope, review contributions, enforce security and conduct policy, and are accountable for evidence-backed releases.

Public issues and discussions are the default decision forum. Accepted consequential decisions are recorded as ADRs. Breaking protocol changes require an RFC issue with motivation, compatibility impact, migration plan, security analysis, alternatives, and rollout evidence.

Maintainers may appoint additional maintainers based on sustained technical judgment, review quality, community conduct, and reliable stewardship. Conflicts of interest must be disclosed. Security reports and conduct cases may be handled privately.

Hardproof follows Semantic Versioning. External contribution branches are squash-merged by default. No telemetry contribution is accepted without an explicit public RFC, an opt-in design, privacy review, and a way to operate fully without telemetry.

The project's working name remains subject to brand clearance. Governance must not imply affiliation with Atlassian or imitate its branding.
